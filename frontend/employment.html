Food & Nourishment

  • First United Food Security Access to community meals, groceries, and low-barrier food support in a welcoming space. Verified: April 2026
  • Guru Nanak's Free Kitchen Providing free, nutritious vegetarian meals to anyone in need without judgment. Verified: April 2026
  • Crabtree Corner (YWCA) A community hub offering hot meals and support services specifically for women and families.
  • Women’s Health Collective Access to nutrition, health resources, and a safe space dedicated to women's well-being.
  • Downtown Eastside Women’s Centre (DEWC) A self-identified women-only space providing hot meals, basic needs, and a safe sanctuary.

Employment & Digital Access

  • WorkBC Employment Services Access personal job coaching, skills training, and financial supports to help you return to the workforce.
  • S.U.C.C.E.S.S. WorkBC Centres Specialized employment support and workshops available in multiple languages.
  • VPL Computer Booking Secure a private computer station with internet access for job searching, banking, or personal tasks.
  • EMBERS Eastside Works A low-barrier income generation hub helping you find day labor or long-term employment.
  • VPL "Get Connected" Program One-on-one sessions to learn technology, improve ESL skills, or meet with a social worker.

LGBTQ2S+ Support & Connection

  • QMUNITY BC BC's queer, trans, and two-spirit resource centre offering counselling, support groups, and programs for all ages.
  • Queer Youth Inclusion (QYI) City-led programs and resources designed to create safe, inclusive recreation spaces for queer youth.
  • What’s On Queer BC: Youth & Families A comprehensive directory of community events and specialized resources for youth and their families.

Indigenous Support & Reconciliation

  • Vancouver Aboriginal Friendship Centre (VAFCS) Providing holistic social services, community events, and cultural programs for urban Indigenous people.
  • BWSS Indigenous Women’s Programs Culturally grounded support, advocacy, and healing circles for Indigenous women.
  • Aboriginal Front Door A culturally safe environment providing peer support, food, and traditional healing.
  • Indigenous Rights & Reconciliation Curated reading lists, local event info, and resources centered on Indigenous culture.

Movement & Public Spaces

  • Leisure Access Program Provides subsidized access to community centers, pools, and rinks to support movement and health.
  • Stanley Park Seawall A 10km paved path for walking or resting with accessible washrooms and covered areas for shelter.
  • Oppenheimer Park A community hub offering social programs, a field house, and a safe place for outdoor connection.
  • CRAB Park at Portside Waterfront green space with mountain views, located conveniently near local support services.

Children & Youth Programming

  • VPL Teen Programs Creative workshops, homework help, and social spaces for youth to connect.
  • VPL Kids & Family Programs Storytimes, play-based learning, and educational events for children.
  • Red Fox Healthy Living Active play and leadership training in local parks targeted toward youth and children.

Community & Cultural Sharing

  • VPL Community Events Regular events including movie screenings, music, and community building workshops.
  • Gordon Neighbourhood House A place to connect with neighbors through community herb gardens, low-cost markets, and social clubs.
  • Carnegie Community Centre A "living room" for the community offering free educational programs, arts, and low-cost cafeteria meals.
  • Kiwassa Neighbourhood House A non-profit hub providing free community kitchens, tax clinics, and volunteer-led social activities.
  • Vancouver Urban Food Forest Network A collective focused on edible landscaping and community gardening where neighbors can learn to grow and share food for free.
